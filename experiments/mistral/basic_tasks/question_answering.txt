General Relativity is a theory of gravity that was developed by Albert Einstein between 1907 and 1915. Here's a simplified explanation:

1. Gravitation: We all know that large objects like planets pull smaller objects towards them, which we call gravity. In Newton's law of universal gravitation, this force is caused by the mass of the objects and their distance apart. However, Einstein wanted to find a theory where gravity isn't just a "force" but a manifestation of the curvature of space-time (the fabric of the universe).

2. Space-Time: In General Relativity, space and time are not separate entities but rather combined into a four-dimensional "fabric" known as space-time. This fabric is flexible, capable of being curved or warped by mass and energy.

3. Mass-Energy Equivalence: One of the key principles in General Relativity is the equivalence of mass and energy, as expressed in Einstein's famous equation E=mc^2. This means that mass can be converted into energy and vice versa.

4. Gravitational Field: When an object with mass (like a planet) exists within this space-time fabric, it causes the fabric to warp or curve around it. This curved region is called the gravitational field. Other objects moving in this space-time will follow the curvature of the field, making them appear to "fall" towards the massive object.

5. Einstein's Field Equations: To describe how the curvature of space-time (gravitational field) is influenced by mass and energy, Einstein developed a set of equations known as the Einstein Field Equations. These complex mathematical expressions are used to predict the shape of the gravitational field around objects based on their mass and distribution.

6. Predictions: General Relativity makes various predictions, such as the bending of light by gravity (gravitational lensing), the slowing down of time near massive objects (gravity redshift), and the existence of black holes and wormholes. Many of these predictions have been confirmed through experiments and observations over the past century.

In summary, General Relativity describes gravity as the curvature of space-time caused by mass and energy. Instead of a "force" acting between two objects, they move along the warped fabric of space-time due to its curvature.
