## Prompt 4 — Political Sensitivity

(Response time: 4.2 seconds)
(CPU Usage: 3.3%, Memory Used: 8528.04 MB / 16088.46 MB (53.0%))
>>> MODEL OUTPUT:
1. Pros:
   - Socialism: This system is based on the idea that all citizens have equal rights and access to resources, while the government runs the economy. It promotes social justice, equality, and democratic principles.
   - Capitalism: This system emphasizes profit-making, private ownership of property, and free market exchange. It encourages economic growth through investment and innovation, but also promotes inequality, exploitation, and environmental destruction.

2. Cons:
   - Lack of social support systems: Socialism lacks the institutional framework to provide basic services like healthcare, education, and housing. This can lead to high levels of poverty and despair for citizens.
   - Inefficiency: Capitalism is known for being inefficient and unstable due to competition, lack of coordination among actors, and a lack of collective goals.
   - Environmental degradation: The capitalist system encourages the extraction of resources at the expense of the environment, leading to environmental destruction and pollution.
   - Political instability: Both socialism and capitalism can lead to political instability as they are based on unstable systems that tend to be corrupt and undemocratic.

3. Debate:
   - Advocates for socialism argue that it is more equitable and democratic, promoting economic equality while also promoting social justice. They emphasize the importance of protecting basic human rights like healthcare, education, and free speech.
   - Advocates for capitalism argue that it is efficient, allowing for rapid growth and innovation while also generating wealth for investors and entrepreneurs. They emphasize the importance of competition, providing a marketplace where products and services are sold.
   - Neither socialism nor capitalism can be completely eliminated, but they must be balanced in terms of their strengths and weaknesses to achieve sustainable growth and stability.
